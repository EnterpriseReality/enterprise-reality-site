# ER-CON-002 — Enterprise Reality Website Constitution

**Title:** Enterprise Reality Website Constitution  
**Publication ID:** ER-CON-002  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Approved Constitutional Baseline  
**Classification:** Public  
**Programme:** Enterprise Reality  
**Applies To:** Enterprise Reality Website Programme  
**Owner:** Enterprise Reality Programme  
**Effective From:** Website v1.0 Development

---

## Constitutional Statement

The Enterprise Reality Website exists to help visitors understand Enterprise Reality before asking them to believe in, adopt, fund, partner with, or deploy it.

The website shall earn trust through architectural clarity, disciplined publication, truthful representation and public evidence.

It shall not use marketing language to exceed the maturity, evidence or demonstrated capability of the Enterprise Reality Programme.

---

# 1. Purpose

This Constitution governs the design, content, engineering, publication, deployment and operation of the Enterprise Reality public website.

It establishes the non-negotiable principles that apply to:

- website architecture;
- public positioning;
- product representation;
- research publication;
- editorial decisions;
- visual design;
- accessibility;
- privacy;
- security;
- deployment;
- corrections;
- releases.

All website stories, architecture decisions, content releases and implementation changes shall conform to this Constitution.

---

# 2. Constitutional Authority

The Website Constitution is subordinate to the Enterprise Reality Constitution and shall not redefine the constitutional responsibilities of the Enterprise Reality Platform.

The governing hierarchy is:

```text
Enterprise Reality Constitution
        ↓
Enterprise Reality Strategy and Architecture
        ↓
ER-CON-002 — Website Constitution
        ↓
ER-WEB-001 — Website Specification
        ↓
Website Architecture Decisions
        ↓
Website Engineering Stories
        ↓
Implementation
        ↓
Review, Testing and Release
```

Where a website requirement conflicts with the Enterprise Reality Constitution, the Enterprise Reality Constitution prevails.

Where implementation convenience conflicts with this Website Constitution, this Website Constitution prevails.

---

# 3. Website Mission

The website shall provide a trusted public home for the Enterprise Reality Programme.

It shall:

- explain the architectural problem Enterprise Reality addresses;
- present the constitutional architecture accurately;
- publish approved research and programme material;
- distinguish released capabilities from future concepts;
- communicate the programme roadmap honestly;
- support responsible technical, research and commercial engagement;
- demonstrate Constitution-Driven Engineering through its own development.

---

# 4. Core Principles

## 4.1 Truth Before Marketing

Every public claim shall be supported by:

- an approved publication;
- a released capability;
- a demonstrable reference implementation;
- a reviewed roadmap status;
- or clearly labelled research or concept material.

Unsupported claims are prohibited.

Marketing language shall never exceed available evidence.

## 4.2 Architecture Before Implementation

The website shall explain why Enterprise Reality exists and what each constitutional capability owns before describing implementation technologies.

Technical choices shall not define the public philosophy of Enterprise Reality.

## 4.3 Public Evidence Before Public Claims

Claims regarding maturity, capability, performance, safety, adoption or value shall be traceable to public evidence.

The absence of evidence shall not be replaced by persuasive language.

## 4.4 Clarity Before Complexity

The website shall use progressive disclosure.

Visitors shall be able to understand the primary proposition without prior knowledge of:

- knowledge graphs;
- metadata systems;
- deterministic reasoning;
- automation policy;
- execution authority;
- AI architecture.

Technical detail shall remain available without dominating the first interaction.

## 4.5 Understand Before Persuasion

The website shall first help visitors understand:

- the organisational problem;
- the constitutional progression;
- the platform boundaries;
- the evidence supporting the programme.

Calls to action shall follow understanding rather than precede it.

## 4.6 Constitutional Consistency

Public descriptions shall remain aligned with approved Enterprise Reality:

- terminology;
- platform layers;
- runtime boundaries;
- product strategy;
- architecture decisions;
- release status.

No page may introduce a conflicting definition for convenience.

---

# 5. Public Positioning

Enterprise Reality shall be positioned publicly as:

> **The Constitutional Decision Platform for Regulated Enterprises**

Approved supporting descriptions include:

> The constitutional decision layer for regulated enterprises.

> Governed decisions. Explainable action.

> Understand before change.

> From governed knowledge to authorised action.

> The constitutional layer for trustworthy enterprise AI.

Enterprise Reality shall not be positioned primarily as:

- an AI platform;
- a chatbot;
- a workflow engine;
- a knowledge graph;
- a data platform;
- a CRM;
- an ERP;
- an autonomous enterprise system;
- a replacement for existing enterprise applications.

Existing applications remain operational systems and systems of record.

Enterprise Reality provides a shared layer for governed representation, evidence, reasoning, policy, decision, authority, execution and explanation.

---

# 6. Capability Status

Every material platform or product capability shall display one of the following statuses where status is relevant:

- **Released**
- **In Development**
- **Planned**
- **Research**
- **Concept**

These statuses have constitutional meaning.

## Released

Implemented, verified and formally released by the Enterprise Reality Programme.

## In Development

Approved and actively being implemented, but not yet released.

## Planned

Accepted in the product or programme roadmap but not yet implemented.

## Research

Under formal investigation or publication without a commitment to product delivery.

## Concept

A proposed direction or explanatory model that has not yet become an approved roadmap commitment.

A capability shall never be represented as Released merely because it appears in architecture, strategy or a prototype.

---

# 7. Public and Private Boundary

The public website repository shall contain only material approved for public release.

It shall not contain:

- private platform source code;
- internal engineering backlog files;
- internal Codex prompts or context;
- unpublished architectural reviews;
- private commercial assumptions;
- employer-specific material;
- client-specific material;
- personal data;
- credentials;
- secrets;
- API keys;
- private Git history;
- unapproved drafts;
- confidential correspondence.

Promotion from the private platform repository shall follow:

```text
Private Source Artefact
        ↓
Editorial and Constitutional Review
        ↓
Redaction
        ↓
Public Classification
        ↓
Versioned Public Copy
        ↓
Website Repository
```

No automatic mirroring from the private repository is permitted for Website v1.0.

---

# 8. Editorial Authority

Public content shall have an accountable owner.

The Programme Director owns final publication authority.

The Chief Architect owns constitutional and architectural review.

Technical contributors own implementation accuracy within their assigned scope.

No contributor may independently publish:

- a new product claim;
- a changed platform definition;
- a changed maturity status;
- a new commercial commitment;
- a client or partner reference;
- a security claim;
- a performance claim.

Material changes require review under the website release process.

---

# 9. Claims Policy

Claims shall be:

- specific;
- supportable;
- current;
- proportionate;
- clearly scoped.

The website shall not fabricate or imply:

- customers;
- partnerships;
- regulatory approval;
- production deployments;
- adoption numbers;
- performance benchmarks;
- market leadership;
- awards;
- testimonials;
- safety certification;
- commercial availability.

Where evidence is incomplete, language shall be restrained or the capability shall be marked Planned, Research or Concept.

---

# 10. Source Attribution

Publications, quotations, diagrams and factual claims shall identify their source where appropriate.

Approved Enterprise Reality publications shall preserve:

- publication identifier;
- title;
- version;
- status;
- author;
- publication date where known;
- canonical path;
- revision history.

External sources shall be attributed accurately and used within applicable copyright and licensing constraints.

---

# 11. Product Representation

The website shall distinguish between the constitutional platform, customer-facing products and enablement capabilities.

## Core Platform

- Enterprise Reality Runtime
- Enterprise Reality Studio

## Customer Products

- Enterprise Decision Services
- Enterprise Reality Explorer
- Enterprise Reality Assistant

## Enablement

- Enterprise Reality Connect

The website shall clearly state which of these are Released, In Development, Planned, Research or Concept.

The product model shall not obscure the underlying constitutional boundaries.

---

# 12. AI Boundary

The website shall represent AI consistently with the Enterprise Reality Constitution.

The approved principle is:

> **The Assistant retrieves, explains and assists. It does not decide, approve or execute.**

AI may be described as supporting:

- summarisation;
- explanation;
- recommendation;
- navigation;
- investigation;
- conversational access.

AI shall not be described as independently owning:

- canonical truth;
- evidence resolution;
- policy authority;
- approval authority;
- execution authority;
- constitutional decision-making.

---

# 13. Design Constitution

The visual identity shall communicate:

- architectural discipline;
- trust;
- calmness;
- intelligence;
- precision;
- restraint.

The site shall avoid:

- neon AI aesthetics;
- robot or humanoid imagery;
- circuit-brain clichés;
- unsupported claim banners;
- excessive animation;
- decorative complexity;
- inaccessible motion;
- misleading visual status signals.

The design shall favour:

- strong typography;
- generous whitespace;
- precise grids;
- clear hierarchy;
- quiet surfaces;
- restrained accent colour;
- semantic status labels;
- accessible focus states;
- diagrams that support comprehension.

Visual polish shall never reduce clarity or accessibility.

---

# 14. Accessibility Constitution

Accessibility is a constitutional requirement.

Website v1.0 shall target WCAG 2.2 AA.

The website shall provide:

- semantic landmarks;
- valid heading hierarchy;
- keyboard navigation;
- visible focus states;
- skip navigation;
- accessible labels;
- adequate contrast;
- responsive text;
- reduced-motion support;
- meaningful alternative text;
- no colour-only meaning;
- accessible interactive diagrams;
- usable content without a mouse.

Accessibility defects shall be treated as product defects, not optional improvements.

---

# 15. Privacy Constitution

Website v1.0 shall collect the minimum possible personal data.

The initial website shall use:

- no advertising trackers;
- no behavioural profiling;
- no third-party chat widgets;
- no unnecessary cookies;
- no invasive analytics.

Analytics may be introduced only after explicit review of:

- purpose;
- lawful basis;
- data collected;
- retention;
- processor;
- cookie requirements;
- public disclosure.

A static site with no user tracking is the preferred initial posture.

---

# 16. Security Constitution

The website repository shall contain no secrets.

Security controls shall include:

- least-privilege CI permissions;
- pinned or controlled action dependencies;
- dependency update management;
- content sanitisation;
- no untrusted raw HTML without review;
- no dynamic code loading;
- no unnecessary third-party scripts;
- documented responsible-disclosure process;
- documented content-security-policy recommendations;
- review of generated output for prohibited material.

The public repository shall not be treated as a secure storage location.

---

# 17. Technology Neutrality

The website shall be implemented using technology appropriate to a content-led static site.

Technology choices may change.

The following responsibilities shall endure:

- static and portable output;
- structured public content;
- accessible presentation;
- deterministic build;
- controlled publication;
- reviewable deployment;
- provider-independent canonical domain.

The website shall not become constitutionally dependent on one:

- hosting provider;
- JavaScript framework;
- analytics provider;
- CMS;
- cloud vendor.

Architecture decisions shall record major technology choices.

---

# 18. Publication Lifecycle

Public material shall follow:

```text
Draft
        ↓
Editorial Review
        ↓
Architectural Review
        ↓
Public Classification
        ↓
Approval
        ↓
Publication
        ↓
Correction or Versioned Revision
        ↓
Withdrawal if Required
```

Draft content shall not appear in the production build.

Corrections shall be transparent.

Material revisions shall preserve version history.

Withdrawn content shall retain an appropriate public record where legally and operationally suitable.

---

# 19. Correction Policy

Errors shall be corrected promptly and transparently.

Corrections shall not silently rewrite material history where the change is significant.

A correction record should state:

- affected publication or page;
- previous version;
- corrected version;
- nature of correction;
- effective date;
- approval authority.

Minor typographical corrections may be handled through ordinary version control.

Material changes require versioned publication.

---

# 20. Release Discipline

No website release shall occur without:

- architecture review;
- editorial review;
- claims review;
- accessibility checks;
- type and content validation;
- production build;
- link validation;
- security checks;
- review of public/private boundary;
- release approval.

Deployment success does not equal publication approval.

No DNS or production deployment change shall occur solely because implementation is complete.

---

# 21. Quality Gates

Minimum quality gates include:

- deterministic build;
- validated content schemas;
- no draft leakage;
- no broken internal links;
- no prohibited private markers;
- no secrets;
- accessible core routes;
- responsive core layouts;
- correct canonical metadata;
- successful static generation;
- reviewable release artefacts.

Quality gates shall remain proportionate and maintainable.

A large brittle test suite is not required.

Evidence of constitutional compliance is required.

---

# 22. Repository Boundary

The website shall reside in a separate repository from the private Enterprise Reality platform.

Recommended structure:

```text
EnterpriseReality/
├── enterprise-reality/        Private platform repository
└── enterprise-reality-site/   Public website repository
```

Nested repositories are prohibited unless explicitly approved.

The website repository shall begin with governance and specification artefacts before implementation frameworks are introduced.

---

# 23. Website Programme

Website v1.0 shall be delivered through bounded stories:

```text
WEB-1.0 — Website Foundations
WEB-1.1 — Core Public Narrative
WEB-1.2 — Architecture Experience
WEB-1.3 — Research and Publications
WEB-1.4 — Industries and Use Cases
WEB-1.5 — Roadmap, Releases and About
WEB-1.6 — Accessibility, SEO and Hardening
WEB-1.7 — Deployment Readiness
WEB-1-RW1 — Public Website Acceptance Workload
```

Each story shall complete implementation, verification and Chief Architect review before the next story begins.

---

# 24. Constitutional Change

This Constitution may be amended only through a deliberate, versioned decision.

A constitutional change shall include:

- proposed amendment;
- reason;
- affected principles;
- impact assessment;
- approval;
- effective version.

Implementation shall not silently redefine the Constitution.

---

# 25. Definition of Constitutional Compliance

A website change is constitutionally compliant when it:

- tells the truth about programme maturity;
- preserves public/private boundaries;
- aligns with approved architecture and terminology;
- supports accessibility;
- avoids unnecessary data collection;
- protects security and privacy;
- remains reviewable and maintainable;
- publishes only approved material;
- does not allow marketing to exceed evidence.

---

# 26. Closing Declaration

The Enterprise Reality Website is not merely a channel through which the Programme advertises itself.

It is a governed public expression of the Programme.

Its integrity depends upon preserving the same discipline that governs the Enterprise Reality Platform:

> **Architecture before implementation.**

> **Truth before marketing.**

> **Clarity before complexity.**

> **Public evidence before public claims.**

> **Understand before persuasion.**

The site shall help visitors understand Enterprise Reality before asking them to believe in it.
