# ER-WEB-004 --- Enterprise Reality Website Publication Guide

**Publication ID:** ER-WEB-004\
**Version:** 1.0\
**Status:** Approved Publication Guide\
**Classification:** Public\
**Programme:** Enterprise Reality

---

# Executive Summary

This guide defines how information becomes an approved public
publication within the Enterprise Reality Website.

It governs publication lifecycle, content collections, metadata,
editorial workflow, versioning and the public/private boundary. Its
purpose is to ensure that every published artefact is accurate,
reviewable and constitutionally consistent.

---

# 1. Purpose

The Publication Guide ensures that the website remains a trusted source
of architectural, research and product information.

It provides a repeatable publication process that preserves
truthfulness, traceability and long-term maintainability.

---

# 2. Publication Principles

Every publication shall be:

- truthful;
- evidence-based;
- versioned;
- attributable;
- reviewable;
- accessible;
- discoverable.

Draft material shall never appear in a production release.

---

# 3. Publication Types

The website supports the following publication families:

- Constitutions
- Strategy
- Architecture
- Research
- Guides
- Releases
- Roadmaps
- Industry Scenarios
- News (future)

Each family shall maintain a consistent structure and metadata model.

---

# 4. Content Collections

Structured content collections shall be used for:

- research/
- architecture/
- strategy/
- releases/
- roadmap/
- industries/

Each collection shall validate required metadata during the build.

---

# 5. Publication Metadata

Every publication shall include:

- Publication Identifier
- Title
- Subtitle (where applicable)
- Version
- Status
- Category
- Summary
- Author
- Publication Date
- Updated Date (optional)
- Canonical Path
- Draft Flag
- Featured Flag (optional)

Duplicate identifiers are prohibited.

---

# 6. Publication Lifecycle

```text
Author
    ↓
Draft
    ↓
Editorial Review
    ↓
Chief Architect Review
    ↓
Programme Approval
    ↓
Published
    ↓
Versioned Revision
    ↓
Archive (if required)
```

Every significant revision shall produce a new version history entry.

---

# 7. Editorial Workflow

Editorial review confirms:

- factual accuracy;
- grammar and readability;
- constitutional terminology;
- release status;
- references;
- accessibility.

Architectural review confirms:

- consistency with Enterprise Reality;
- ownership boundaries;
- platform terminology;
- constitutional compliance.

---

# 8. Public / Private Boundary

The public repository shall contain only approved material.

It shall never contain:

- internal engineering backlog;
- unpublished prompts;
- employer or client information;
- credentials or secrets;
- proprietary implementation details;
- private review discussions.

Promotion from the private repository requires review and explicit
approval.

---

# 9. Versioning

Publications use semantic document versioning.

Major versions indicate structural or constitutional change.

Minor versions indicate approved enhancements.

Patch versions indicate corrections.

Previous versions remain traceable.

---

# 10. Release Process

A publication may be released only after:

- editorial approval;
- architectural approval;
- metadata validation;
- successful static build;
- link validation;
- accessibility checks.

Deployment alone does not constitute publication approval.

---

# 11. Quality Gates

Publication quality includes:

- valid metadata;
- no draft leakage;
- working internal links;
- accessible structure;
- correct status labels;
- canonical URLs;
- version history;
- public/private compliance.

---

# 12. Future Evolution

Future releases may introduce:

- multilingual publications;
- printable editions;
- citation exports;
- publication search;
- taxonomy improvements.

These enhancements shall preserve backward compatibility for published
content.

---

# Closing Statement

The Enterprise Reality Website is first and foremost a publication
platform.

Its authority comes not from the volume of content it contains, but from
the discipline with which every publication is reviewed, versioned and
released.
