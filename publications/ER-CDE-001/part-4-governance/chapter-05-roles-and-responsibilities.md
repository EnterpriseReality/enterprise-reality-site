# Chapter 5 — Roles and Responsibilities

> *"Constitutional integrity depends not only upon what is done, but upon who is authorised to do it."*

---

## Purpose

This chapter establishes the constitutional roles and responsibilities governing Constitution-Driven Engineering.

The purpose of these roles is to preserve clear constitutional authority throughout the engineering publication lifecycle.

Each role possesses distinct responsibilities.

No role shall assume the constitutional authority of another.

This separation preserves the integrity, objectivity and trustworthiness of engineering publications.

---

## Constitutional Principle

Constitution-Driven Engineering distinguishes between constitutional authority and engineering execution.

Constitutional authority determines what shall be published.

Engineering execution determines how that publication is faithfully implemented within the engineering repository.

These responsibilities shall remain independent.

---

# Constitutional Role Model

Every engineering publication shall operate within the following constitutional authority structure.

```text
Programme Director
        │
        ▼
Chief Architect
        │
        ▼
Lead Software Engineer
        │
        ▼
Reviewer
        │
        ▼
Publication Steward
```

Each role derives its responsibilities from constitutional governance while remaining accountable for its own decisions.

---

# Programme Director

## Responsibility

The Programme Director provides strategic direction for the publication programme.

The Programme Director determines:

- publication priorities;
- programme objectives;
- publication sequencing;
- constitutional scope;
- publication approval at programme level.

The Programme Director commissions publications but does not author constitutional content unless acting in another authorised role.

---

## Constitutional Authority

The Programme Director authorises the initiation and completion of constitutional engineering work.

The Programme Director shall not modify approved manuscripts outside the constitutional review process.

---

# Chief Architect

## Responsibility

The Chief Architect is the constitutional authority for engineering publications.

The Chief Architect establishes:

- constitutional principles;
- publication scope;
- approved manuscripts;
- constitutional boundaries;
- engineering meaning;
- constitutional reviews.

Approved manuscripts are authored solely by the Chief Architect.

The Approved Manuscript is the constitutional source of truth from which repository publications derive their authority.

---

## Constitutional Authority

The Chief Architect alone is authorised to:

- establish constitutional wording;
- define constitutional intent;
- approve engineering manuscripts;
- approve constitutional reviews;
- approve publication readiness.

The Chief Architect shall not delegate constitutional authority through informal engineering interpretation.

---

# Lead Software Engineer

## Responsibility

The Lead Software Engineer faithfully implements approved constitutional manuscripts within the engineering repository.

Implementation responsibilities include:

- repository authoring;
- publication integration;
- metadata updates;
- repository validation;
- engineering verification;
- evidence collection.

The Lead Software Engineer implements constitutional authority.

The Lead Software Engineer does not establish constitutional authority.

---

## Constitutional Authority

The Lead Software Engineer shall not:

- introduce new constitutional principles;
- reinterpret approved manuscripts;
- expand constitutional scope;
- modify constitutional meaning.

Where uncertainty exists, clarification shall be sought from the Chief Architect before implementation proceeds.

---

# Reviewer

## Responsibility

The Reviewer independently assesses engineering publications against constitutional authority and engineering evidence.

Review activities include:

- manuscript verification;
- evidence validation;
- consistency assessment;
- engineering accuracy;
- publication quality.

Review provides assurance.

It does not establish constitutional authority.

---

## Constitutional Authority

Reviewers may recommend changes.

Only the Chief Architect may approve constitutional changes.

---

# Publication Steward

## Responsibility

The Publication Steward preserves the integrity of the published engineering canon.

Responsibilities include:

- publication indexing;
- version history;
- publication metadata;
- release records;
- publication availability;
- canonical references.

The Publication Steward manages publication assets rather than constitutional content.

---

## Constitutional Authority

The Publication Steward shall not modify constitutional meaning during publication maintenance.

Administrative stewardship shall remain independent of constitutional authorship.

---

# Separation of Responsibilities

Constitution-Driven Engineering intentionally separates constitutional authority from engineering implementation.

This separation provides:

- independent governance;
- objective review;
- publication traceability;
- constitutional accountability;
- engineering integrity.

No individual role shall assume responsibilities assigned to another constitutional role without explicit constitutional authorisation.

# Relationship to the Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle

The responsibilities defined within this chapter correspond directly to the Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle established in Chapter 4.

Each lifecycle stage is owned by an authorised constitutional role.

Responsibility therefore follows constitutional authority rather than organisational hierarchy.

---

# Relationship to the Canonical Laws

The constitutional roles exist to exercise the Canonical Laws.

They do not replace them.

Every constitutional responsibility shall therefore remain consistent with the obligations established in Chapter 3.

---

# Enduring Principle

Constitution-Driven Engineering preserves engineering trust by separating constitutional authority from engineering implementation.

Every role contributes to the publication lifecycle.

Only together do they preserve the constitutional integrity of the engineering canon.
