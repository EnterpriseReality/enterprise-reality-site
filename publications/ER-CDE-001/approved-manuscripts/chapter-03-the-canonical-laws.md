# Chapter 3 — The Canonical Laws

> *"Constitutions do not create truth. They preserve it."*

---

## Purpose

This chapter establishes the Canonical Laws of Constitution-Driven Engineering.

These laws define the enduring constitutional obligations governing the creation, review and publication of engineering knowledge.

They were not conceived as theoretical principles.

They emerged through the practical application of constitutional engineering during the development of the Enterprise Reality Website Engineering Handbook (ER-WEB-005).

Each law represents an engineering discipline that proved necessary to preserve truth, evidence and trust throughout the publication lifecycle.

Collectively, they define the constitutional behaviour expected of every engineering publication produced under the Enterprise Reality Programme.

---

## Constitutional Nature of the Laws

The Canonical Laws are cumulative.

No individual law shall be interpreted in isolation.

Each derives meaning from the constitutional foundations established in Chapters 1 and 2 and reinforces the integrity of the laws that precede it.

These laws govern engineering publications.

They do not replace software engineering practices, architectural principles or implementation standards.

---

# CDE-001 — Engineering Traceability

### Constitutional Statement

Every engineering statement shall be traceable to identifiable implementation evidence.

### Constitutional Meaning

Engineering publications derive authority from observable implementation rather than opinion or assumption.

### Required Behaviour

Every engineering claim shall be supported by repository evidence.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Engineering publications shall not contain assertions that cannot be traced to identifiable implementation evidence.

### Practical Example

ER-WEB-005 documented implemented Astro components only after repository inspection confirmed their existence.

---

# CDE-002 — Evidence Supremacy

### Constitutional Statement

Observable engineering evidence shall always take precedence over assumption, opinion or expectation.

### Constitutional Meaning

Engineering publications describe implemented reality.

### Required Behaviour

Where repository evidence and documentation differ, repository evidence shall prevail.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Documentation shall not be preserved merely because it existed previously.

### Practical Example

Repository inspection determined publication content throughout ER-WEB-005.

---

# CDE-003 — Framework Neutrality

### Constitutional Statement

Engineering publications shall describe implemented behaviour rather than framework capability.

### Constitutional Meaning

Framework documentation and engineering documentation serve different purposes.

### Required Behaviour

Only implemented framework behaviour shall be documented.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Engineering publications shall not become framework tutorials.

### Practical Example

The Astro chapters described the implemented website rather than the Astro framework itself.

---

# CDE-004 — Design Fidelity

### Constitutional Statement

Engineering design shall be documented exactly as implemented.

### Constitutional Meaning

Engineering publications faithfully describe realised design.

### Required Behaviour

Published design descriptions shall correspond to implemented behaviour.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Future architectural aspirations shall not be represented as implemented design.

### Practical Example

Repository architecture chapters reflected the implemented repository rather than planned future evolution.

---

# CDE-005 — Presentation Fidelity

### Constitutional Statement

Presentation engineering shall be documented exactly as implemented.

### Constitutional Meaning

Presentation descriptions remain subordinate to observable implementation.

### Required Behaviour

Only implemented presentation behaviour shall be described.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Visual intent shall not be inferred where implementation evidence is absent.

### Practical Example

Website styling was documented solely from implemented CSS and rendered behaviour.

---

# CDE-006 — Knowledge Organisation

### Constitutional Statement

Engineering knowledge shall be organised according to the implemented constitutional publication structure.

### Constitutional Meaning

Knowledge is itself governed.

### Required Behaviour

Engineering publications shall follow the constitutional publication hierarchy.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Engineering knowledge shall not become structurally inconsistent through uncontrolled publication.

### Practical Example

ER-WEB-005 maintained a consistent hierarchy of Parts, Chapters and publication indexes.

---

# CDE-007 — Discoverability

### Constitutional Statement

Governed engineering knowledge shall remain discoverable through implemented navigation.

### Constitutional Meaning

Navigation is an engineering responsibility.

### Required Behaviour

Discoverability mechanisms shall faithfully represent implemented navigation structures.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Engineering publications shall not describe navigation that does not exist.

### Practical Example

Navigation chapters documented implemented menus, routing and publication indexes.

---

# CDE-008 — Identity Through Metadata

### Constitutional Statement

Metadata shall establish the constitutional identity of every engineering publication.

### Constitutional Meaning

Metadata preserves identity, governance and traceability.

### Required Behaviour

Canonical metadata shall be maintained consistently throughout the publication lifecycle.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Metadata shall not be treated as optional publication decoration.

### Practical Example

Every ER-WEB-005 chapter carried governed publication metadata.

---

# CDE-009 — Discoverability Follows Identity

### Constitutional Statement

Discoverability shall expose engineering knowledge without becoming its source of authority.

### Constitutional Meaning

Identity always precedes discovery.

### Required Behaviour

Canonical publication identity shall exist independently of search mechanisms.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Search optimisation shall not redefine publication authority.

### Practical Example

Publication identifiers existed independently of website navigation and search indexing.

---

# CDE-010 — Semantic Fidelity

### Constitutional Statement

Machine-readable representations shall faithfully reflect governed engineering publications.

### Constitutional Meaning

Structured representations communicate existing meaning.

They do not create new meaning.

### Required Behaviour

Structured metadata shall remain consistent with the published engineering content.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Machine-readable representations shall not introduce semantics absent from the publication.

### Practical Example

JSON-LD reflected implemented publication metadata without extending it.

---

# CDE-011 — Inclusive Engineering

### Constitutional Statement

Engineering publications shall remain accessible through demonstrably implemented accessibility mechanisms.

### Constitutional Meaning

Accessibility is an engineering responsibility supported by observable implementation.

### Required Behaviour

Accessibility claims shall be supported by implementation evidence and validation.

### Prohibited Behaviour

Engineering publications shall not claim accessibility features that have not been implemented or verified.

### Practical Example

Accessibility chapters documented implemented semantic HTML, keyboard support and automated accessibility testing.
