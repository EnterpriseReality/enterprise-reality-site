# Chapter 2 — Constitutional Position

> *"A constitution does not engineer a system. It governs how engineering remains faithful to truth."*

---

## Purpose

This chapter establishes the constitutional position occupied by Constitution-Driven Engineering within the Enterprise Reality Programme.

It defines the relationship between constitutional philosophy, architecture, engineering and implementation, and explains the authority under which engineering publications are created.

The objective is to ensure that engineering knowledge remains faithful to implemented reality while preserving constitutional integrity throughout the lifetime of an engineering system.

---

## Constitutional Position

Constitution-Driven Engineering occupies a specific position within the constitutional hierarchy of the Enterprise Reality Programme.

It is neither an architectural framework nor a software engineering methodology.

Instead, it is the constitutional discipline through which engineering knowledge is created, reviewed, validated and published.

Its authority derives from Enterprise Reality, which establishes the enduring constitutional principles governing truth, evidence and identity.

Constitution-Driven Engineering applies those principles to engineering publications.

It does not redefine them.

---

## Relationship to Enterprise Reality

Enterprise Reality provides the constitutional philosophy of the programme.

It establishes principles such as:

- constitutional authority;
- stable identity;
- evidence-based truth;
- governance;
- stewardship.

These principles exist independently of any individual engineering implementation.

Constitution-Driven Engineering extends those constitutional principles into engineering publication.

Where Enterprise Reality asks:

> *What constitutes truth?*

Constitution-Driven Engineering asks:

> *How should engineering knowledge faithfully represent that truth?*

The relationship is therefore complementary.

Enterprise Reality establishes constitutional philosophy.

Constitution-Driven Engineering establishes constitutional publication practice.

---

## Constitutional Authority

Every engineering publication shall exist under constitutional authority.

Constitutional authority establishes:

- why the publication exists;
- who governs it;
- what it may describe;
- what it may not describe;
- how it may evolve.

Engineering publications are therefore governed documents rather than informal technical notes.

No engineering publication may introduce constitutional principles independently of the constitutional publications from which it derives authority.

---

## Engineering Authority

Engineering authority differs from constitutional authority.

Constitutional authority determines legitimacy.

Engineering authority determines accuracy.

Engineering authority is established through observable implementation evidence including, but not limited to:

- source code;
- repository structure;
- configuration;
- automated testing;
- build outputs;
- deployment artefacts.

Engineering publications remain subordinate to this evidence.

Where evidence changes, engineering publications shall be updated accordingly.

---

## Architecture and Engineering

Architecture and engineering fulfil different constitutional responsibilities.

Architecture defines the enduring structure, responsibilities and boundaries of a system.

It explains what the system is intended to become and how its major responsibilities are organised.

Engineering documents how that architecture has been realised through observable implementation.

Architecture therefore governs engineering.

Engineering demonstrates architecture.

Neither replaces the other.

The two disciplines remain complementary throughout the engineering lifecycle.

---

## Repository Evidence

Within Constitution-Driven Engineering, the engineering repository is regarded as the primary observable source of implementation truth.

Repository evidence includes all artefacts that demonstrate implemented behaviour, including:

- source files;
- configuration files;
- build definitions;
- automated tests;
- validation scripts;
- deployment configuration;
- generated outputs.

Engineering publications shall describe repository evidence faithfully.

They shall not speculate beyond what the repository demonstrates.

---

## Evidence Hierarchy

Engineering publications shall recognise a consistent hierarchy of authority.

```text
Constitution
        │
        ▼
Architecture
        │
        ▼
Engineering Baselines
        │
        ▼
Repository Evidence
        │
        ▼
Engineering Publications
```

Each level derives authority from the level above while remaining accountable to observable evidence below.

Engineering publications therefore occupy the final stage of constitutional communication.

They describe implemented reality.

They do not establish it.

---

## Scope

Constitution-Driven Engineering governs the production of engineering publications.

Its scope includes:

- constitutional engineering publications;
- engineering baselines;
- repository evidence;
- engineering traceability;
- publication governance;
- engineering reviews;
- editorial refinement;
- publication approval.

Its scope explicitly excludes:

- software development methodologies;
- programming language selection;
- framework selection;
- implementation patterns;
- project management;
- engineering certification;
- organisational maturity models;
- commercial consulting methods.

These remain outside the constitutional boundary of this publication.

---

## Core Definitions

For the purposes of Constitution-Driven Engineering, the following definitions apply.

**Constitutional Authority**

The governing authority from which a publication derives its legitimacy.

**Engineering Authority**

The observable implementation evidence upon which engineering accuracy is established.

**Repository Evidence**

Observable artefacts within the engineering repository that demonstrate implemented behaviour.

**Engineering Publication**

A governed publication that faithfully documents observable engineering implementation.

**Canonical Publication**

A publication approved through constitutional review and accepted into the permanent engineering canon.

**Evidence Package**

The documented body of engineering evidence supporting a published engineering chapter or sprint.

**Chief Architect Review**

The constitutional review that validates engineering publications against constitutional principles and approved authority.

**Publication Pass**

The final constitutional activity through which an approved publication is promoted into the permanent canon.

---

## Constitutional Boundaries

Constitution-Driven Engineering deliberately establishes clear constitutional boundaries.

It governs how engineering knowledge is created.

It does not govern how software is engineered.

It governs how engineering evidence is interpreted.

It does not replace implementation.

It governs engineering publications.

It does not replace architecture.

Maintaining these distinctions preserves the constitutional integrity of both Enterprise Reality and Constitution-Driven Engineering.

---

## Enduring Principle

Constitution-Driven Engineering exists to ensure that engineering publications remain constitutionally governed, evidence-based and faithful to implemented reality.

Its purpose is not to create engineering truth.

Its purpose is to preserve engineering truth through disciplined constitutional publication.
