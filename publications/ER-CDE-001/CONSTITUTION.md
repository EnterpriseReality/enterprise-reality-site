# ER-CDE-001 Constitution

**Publication ID:** ER-CDE-001  
**Version:** 0.1.0  
**Status:** In Development  
**Classification:** Constitutional Engineering Doctrine  
**Milestone:** CDE-0 - Publication Foundation  
**Review State:** Foundation Pending Review

## 1. Authority

This Constitution governs the ER-CDE-001 publication repository, catalogue, metadata standard, review lifecycle and public/private boundary.

ER-CDE-001 v1.0.0 may capture only Constitution-Driven Engineering methodology already demonstrated through ER-WEB-005. It must not invent methodology or override evidence from ER-WEB-005.

## 2. Scope

ER-CDE-001 owns the governed structure for a public doctrine publication about Constitution-Driven Engineering as demonstrated by ER-WEB-005.

The v1.0.0 scope excludes:

- certification;
- maturity models;
- commercial positioning;
- training programmes;
- implementation-pattern catalogues;
- tooling frameworks;
- sector-specific variants;
- extensive case studies.

## 3. Evidence Requirements

Claims made by future chapters must be grounded in ER-WEB-005 evidence, approved public repository files or approved public publication records.

Undemonstrated claims, speculative extensions and undocumented methodology are prohibited.

## 4. Review

Publication changes require review appropriate to their scope:

- editorial review for clarity and publication quality;
- constitutional review for scope and authority;
- evidence review for ER-WEB-005 alignment;
- public/private review before approval or publication.

## 5. Versioning

ER-CDE-001 uses semantic document versioning.

- Major versions record approved doctrine publication releases.
- Minor versions record foundation, catalogue or reviewed content milestones.
- Patch versions record corrections that do not materially change meaning.

Version history must be maintained in `ER-CDE-001.md`.

## 6. Canonical-Law Governance

Canonical laws may be documented only where they are already evidenced by ER-WEB-005. CDE-0 does not add, alter or approve canonical laws.

Any future change to canonical-law wording or status requires explicit constitutional review and evidence review.

## 7. Public/Private Boundaries

ER-CDE-001 is public-facing doctrine. It must not include private prompts, private backlog content, unpublished reviews, credentials, local secrets, client or employer information, private platform material or confidential correspondence.

Git is the canonical source of truth for publication content, metadata and approval records.

## 8. Amendment Rules

Amendments must preserve the approved publication catalogue, metadata standard, evidence boundary and public/private boundary. Structural amendments require updates to this Constitution, `ER-CDE-001.md` and `PUBLICATION-INDEX.md` as applicable.

## 9. Completion Boundary

CDE-0 is complete when the governed directory structure, Constitution, master publication file, publication index, metadata template, Part READMEs and supporting directory READMEs exist, all planned chapters and Appendix A remain Planned, and no manuscript files have been created.
