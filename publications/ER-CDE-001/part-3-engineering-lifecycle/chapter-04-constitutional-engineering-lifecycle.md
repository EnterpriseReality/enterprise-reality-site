# Chapter 4 — Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle

> *"A constitutional publication is not produced by a sequence of documents. It is produced by a sequence of governed decisions."*

---

## Purpose

This chapter establishes the Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle of Constitution-Driven Engineering (CDE).

The lifecycle defines the governed process through which engineering knowledge progresses from recognised need to canonical publication.

It provides a repeatable constitutional model for producing engineering publications that remain faithful to implemented reality while preserving constitutional authority, engineering evidence and publication integrity.

The lifecycle is intentionally concise.

Every stage exists because it proved necessary during the constitutional development of engineering publications within the Enterprise Reality Programme.

No stage exists for administrative convenience.

Each stage exists to preserve engineering truth.

---

## Constitutional Principle

The Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle governs engineering publications.

It does not govern software development.

Software engineering methodologies determine how systems are designed and implemented.

Constitution-Driven Engineering determines how engineering knowledge describing those systems is created, validated and preserved.

The lifecycle therefore complements software engineering rather than replacing it.

---

## Constitutional Lifecycle

Every constitutional engineering publication shall progress through the following lifecycle.

```text
Need
        │
        ▼
Constitution
        │
        ▼
Architecture
        │
        ▼
Approved Manuscript
        │
        ▼
Repository Authoring
        │
        ▼
Evidence Package
        │
        ▼
Chief Architect Review
        │
        ▼
Editorial Refinement
        │
        ▼
Publication Pass
        │
        ▼
Canonical Publication
```

No constitutional stage shall be omitted.

The sequence shall remain unchanged unless amended through constitutional governance.

---

# Stage 1 — Need

Every engineering publication begins with a recognised need.

The need may arise from:

- implementation of a new engineering capability;
- architectural evolution;
- operational learning;
- engineering governance;
- publication maintenance;
- constitutional clarification.

The identification of need establishes the purpose of the publication.

It does not establish its content.

---

# Stage 2 — Constitution

Before engineering knowledge is authored, its constitutional authority shall be established.

This stage defines:

- why the publication exists;
- its constitutional scope;
- its boundaries;
- governing authority;
- relationship to existing constitutional publications.

No engineering publication shall proceed without constitutional authority.

---

# Stage 3 — Architecture

Architecture establishes the enduring structure that engineering will describe.

This stage defines:

- architectural responsibilities;
- structural organisation;
- enduring design decisions;
- constitutional relationships.

Architecture establishes what shall be described.

Engineering later documents how those architectural responsibilities have been realised.

---

# Stage 4 — Approved Manuscript

The Approved Manuscript is the constitutional source document for an engineering publication.

It is authored by the Chief Architect.

The Approved Manuscript establishes the authoritative wording, structure and intent of the publication.

Repository chapters derive their authority from the Approved Manuscript.

They do not reinterpret it.

---

# Stage 5 — Repository Authoring

The Lead Software Engineer faithfully authors the repository publication from the Approved Manuscript.

Repository authoring is an implementation activity.

Its purpose is to reproduce constitutional content accurately within the engineering repository.

Repository authoring shall not introduce:

- new constitutional principles;
- additional engineering meaning;
- alternative interpretations;
- editorial enhancements that alter intent.

The Approved Manuscript remains the constitutional authority throughout this stage.

---

# Stage 6 — Evidence Package

Repository authoring produces an Evidence Package.

The Evidence Package demonstrates that:

- constitutional authority has been preserved;
- implementation evidence has been observed;
- validation has succeeded;
- publication integrity has been maintained.

Evidence Packages form part of the permanent constitutional record.

They provide objective evidence supporting engineering publications.

---

# Stage 7 — Chief Architect Review

The Chief Architect performs constitutional review.

The review determines whether:

- constitutional authority has been preserved;
- engineering meaning remains faithful;
- evidence supports published statements;
- publication boundaries have been respected.

The review does not redesign implementation.

It validates constitutional integrity.

---

# Stage 8 — Editorial Refinement

Editorial refinement improves publication quality without altering engineering meaning.

Typical editorial activities include:

- terminology consistency;
- heading normalisation;
- metadata alignment;
- publication numbering;
- formatting consistency;
- cross-reference validation.

Editorial refinement shall never modify constitutional authority or engineering intent.

---

# Stage 9 — Publication Pass

Publication Pass promotes an approved engineering publication into the permanent constitutional canon.

During Publication Pass:

- publication status is updated;
- version information is promoted;
- review history is recorded;
- canonical publication indexes are updated.

Publication Pass is an act of constitutional governance rather than engineering implementation.

---

# Stage 10 — Canonical Publication

A Canonical Publication represents the final constitutional state of an engineering publication.

It becomes:

- constitutionally governed;
- version controlled;
- permanently citable;
- suitable for future constitutional reference.

Canonical Publications may subsequently evolve through future constitutional engineering lifecycles.

Each new version shall repeat the complete constitutional lifecycle.

---

## Lifecycle Characteristics

The Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle possesses the following characteristics.

### Constitutional

Every stage derives authority from constitutional governance.

### Evidence-Based

Engineering publications remain subordinate to observable implementation evidence.

### Traceable

Every published engineering statement can be traced through manuscripts, repository implementation and evidence packages.

### Repeatable

The lifecycle is intended for repeated application across future engineering publications.

### Technology Neutral

The lifecycle governs engineering publications independently of programming languages, frameworks or engineering platforms.

---

## Relationship to the Canonical Laws

The Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle operationalises the Canonical Laws established in Chapter 3.

The lifecycle does not replace those laws.

Instead, it provides the constitutional process through which those laws are exercised during engineering publication.

Every lifecycle stage shall therefore remain consistent with the Canonical Laws.

---

## Enduring Principle

Constitution-Driven Engineering preserves engineering truth through disciplined constitutional governance.

The Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle provides the repeatable constitutional process by which engineering publications remain evidence-based, trustworthy and faithful to implemented reality throughout their lifetime.
