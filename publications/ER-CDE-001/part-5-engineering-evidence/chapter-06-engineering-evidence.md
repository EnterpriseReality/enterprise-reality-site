# Chapter 6 — Engineering Evidence

> *"Engineering publications derive authority from evidence. Without evidence, engineering becomes opinion."*

---

## Purpose

This chapter establishes the constitutional evidence model of Constitution-Driven Engineering.

Engineering evidence provides the objective basis upon which engineering publications are created, reviewed and maintained.

The purpose of engineering evidence is to demonstrate observable implementation rather than intended implementation.

Every engineering publication shall therefore derive its authority from evidence that can be independently verified.

---

## Constitutional Principle

Engineering evidence represents observable engineering reality.

It establishes what has been implemented rather than what was intended, proposed or assumed.

Engineering publications shall faithfully describe that evidence.

Where evidence changes, engineering publications shall evolve accordingly.

Where evidence is absent, engineering publications shall acknowledge its absence.

Engineering evidence therefore remains the constitutional foundation of engineering truth.

---

# Constitutional Evidence Hierarchy

Engineering evidence shall be interpreted according to the following hierarchy.

```text
Repository Evidence
        │
        ▼
Configuration Evidence
        │
        ▼
Build Evidence
        │
        ▼
Validation Evidence
        │
        ▼
Test Evidence
        │
        ▼
Publication Evidence
```

Each form of evidence reinforces the integrity of those that follow.

No publication shall rely upon a lower level of evidence where higher authority evidence is available.

---

# Repository Evidence

Repository Evidence consists of observable artefacts contained within the engineering repository.

Typical examples include:

- source code;
- directory structures;
- configuration files;
- documentation;
- engineering assets;
- build definitions;
- scripts;
- publication content.

Repository Evidence establishes what exists.

It does not explain why it exists.

---

# Configuration Evidence

Configuration Evidence demonstrates how an engineering solution has been configured.

Examples include:

- project configuration;
- build configuration;
- compiler settings;
- routing definitions;
- publication metadata;
- dependency declarations;
- deployment configuration.

Configuration Evidence explains implemented engineering behaviour through observable configuration rather than assumption.

---

# Build Evidence

Build Evidence demonstrates that the engineered solution can be successfully produced from the repository.

Typical Build Evidence includes:

- successful builds;
- generated artefacts;
- compilation output;
- packaging results;
- publication generation;
- boundary validation.

A successful build provides evidence that repository content is internally consistent at a given point in time.

---

# Validation Evidence

Validation Evidence demonstrates compliance with engineering quality controls.

Examples include:

- formatting validation;
- linting;
- static analysis;
- schema validation;
- publication validation;
- boundary validation;
- repository consistency checks.

Validation Evidence demonstrates that engineering standards have been satisfied.

---

# Test Evidence

Test Evidence demonstrates observable engineering behaviour.

Typical Test Evidence includes:

- unit testing;
- integration testing;
- end-to-end testing;
- accessibility testing;
- regression testing.

Successful tests provide confidence that implemented behaviour corresponds to expected behaviour.

They do not replace Repository Evidence.

---

# Publication Evidence

Publication Evidence demonstrates that constitutional engineering governance has been followed.

Publication Evidence includes:

- Approved Manuscripts;
- authored repository chapters;
- Evidence Packages;
- Chief Architect Reviews;
- Editorial Refinement records;
- Publication Pass records;
- publication indexes;
- version history.

Publication Evidence establishes constitutional traceability throughout the engineering publication lifecycle.

---

# Evidence Packages

Every constitutional engineering sprint shall produce an Evidence Package.

The Evidence Package records objective evidence supporting the engineering work completed during that sprint.

Evidence Packages typically include:

- repository verification;
- approved manuscripts;
- authored publications;
- validation results;
- build results;
- repository status;
- explicit constitutional confirmations.

Evidence Packages become permanent constitutional artefacts.

They preserve the engineering history of the publication.

---

# Engineering Verification

Engineering evidence shall be independently verifiable.

Verification methods may include:

- repository inspection;
- successful build execution;
- validation tooling;
- automated testing;
- checksum verification;
- file comparison.

Verification demonstrates that engineering publications faithfully represent implemented reality.

---

# Manuscript Integrity

Approved Manuscripts are the constitutional source documents for engineering publications.

Repository chapters derive their authority from those manuscripts.

Manuscript integrity shall therefore be verified before constitutional review.

Constitution-Driven Engineering recognises two complementary verification mechanisms.

## SHA-256 Verification

Cryptographic hashes demonstrate that two artefacts possess identical content.

Matching SHA-256 values provide strong evidence that an authored chapter faithfully reproduces its Approved Manuscript.

## Byte-for-Byte Verification

Byte-for-byte comparison confirms that no differences exist between the Approved Manuscript and the authored repository chapter.

Tools such as `cmp` provide direct verification of manuscript fidelity.

Both verification methods shall be retained as constitutional engineering practices.

---

# Evidence Before Interpretation

Engineering evidence shall always precede engineering interpretation.

Publications shall not infer behaviour that cannot be demonstrated through observable evidence.

Where interpretation becomes necessary, it shall be explicitly distinguished from observable engineering evidence.

This distinction preserves constitutional trust.

# Relationship to the Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle

Engineering Evidence supports every stage of the Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle.

Repository Authoring produces evidence.

Evidence Packages preserve evidence.

Chief Architect Review evaluates evidence.

Publication Pass records evidence.

Evidence therefore accompanies the publication throughout its constitutional lifecycle.

---

# Enduring Principle

Engineering publications remain trustworthy only while they remain faithful to observable engineering evidence.

Constitution-Driven Engineering therefore treats evidence not as supporting material, but as the constitutional foundation from which engineering truth is derived.
