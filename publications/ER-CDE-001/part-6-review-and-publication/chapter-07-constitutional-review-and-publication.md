# Chapter 7 — Constitutional Review and Publication

> *"A publication becomes canonical not when it is written, but when its constitutional integrity has been independently demonstrated."*

---

## Purpose

This chapter establishes the constitutional governance through which engineering publications are reviewed, refined and accepted into the permanent engineering canon.

Constitution-Driven Engineering recognises that authoring alone is insufficient to establish constitutional authority.

Every engineering publication shall therefore progress through an independent constitutional review before publication.

The objective of review is not to improve engineering implementation.

Its purpose is to verify that constitutional authority, engineering evidence and publication integrity have been faithfully preserved.

---

## Constitutional Principle

Engineering publications shall be governed through independent constitutional review.

No publication shall become canonical solely because it has been authored.

Canonical status is achieved only after constitutional authority has been validated and publication integrity has been demonstrated.

Publication is therefore an act of constitutional governance rather than editorial completion.

---

# Constitutional Publication Model

Every constitutional engineering publication shall follow the same publication governance model.

```text
Chief Architect
        │
        ▼
Approved Manuscript
        │
        ▼
Repository Chapter
        │
        ▼
Evidence Package
        │
        ▼
Chief Architect Review
        │
        ▼
Editorial Refinement
        │
        ▼
Publication Pass
        │
        ▼
Canonical Publication
```

Each stage preserves the constitutional integrity of the publication.

No stage shall be omitted.

---

# Approved Manuscripts

The Approved Manuscript is the constitutional source document for an engineering publication.

It establishes:

- constitutional wording;
- publication structure;
- engineering intent;
- constitutional scope;
- approved terminology.

Repository chapters derive their authority from the Approved Manuscript.

The Approved Manuscript shall remain unchanged throughout repository authoring unless formally amended through constitutional governance.

---

# Repository Chapters

Repository chapters represent the engineering implementation of the Approved Manuscript.

Their purpose is to reproduce constitutional content faithfully within the engineering repository.

Repository chapters shall not introduce:

- new constitutional principles;
- additional engineering meaning;
- altered terminology;
- editorial interpretation affecting constitutional intent.

Repository implementation remains subordinate to the Approved Manuscript.

---

# Evidence Packages

Every constitutional engineering sprint shall conclude with an Evidence Package.

The Evidence Package provides objective evidence that:

- repository work was correctly performed;
- constitutional authority was preserved;
- validation completed successfully;
- engineering integrity was maintained.

Evidence Packages form part of the permanent constitutional record.

They provide future reviewers with traceable engineering history.

---

# Chief Architect Review

Chief Architect Review is the constitutional review stage of the publication lifecycle.

The review independently verifies:

- constitutional authority;
- manuscript fidelity;
- engineering evidence;
- publication scope;
- terminology consistency;
- constitutional boundaries;
- engineering meaning.

Chief Architect Review does not redesign implementation.

Its purpose is to confirm constitutional integrity.

Following review, one of three outcomes shall be recorded:

- Approved;
- Approved with Refinements;
- Returned for Constitutional Revision.

---

# Editorial Refinement

Editorial Refinement improves publication quality without altering constitutional or engineering meaning.

Editorial activities may include:

- terminology normalisation;
- heading consistency;
- metadata alignment;
- numbering consistency;
- publication formatting;
- cross-reference verification;
- grammatical correction.

Editorial refinement shall never modify:

- constitutional principles;
- engineering intent;
- repository evidence;
- Canonical Laws.

Where editorial changes would alter engineering meaning, constitutional review shall be repeated.

---

# Publication Pass

Publication Pass formally promotes an approved engineering publication into the permanent constitutional canon.

Typical Publication Pass activities include:

- publication status promotion;
- version promotion;
- publication history update;
- metadata update;
- publication index update;
- canonical registration.

Publication Pass does not create constitutional authority.

It records that constitutional authority has already been demonstrated.

---

# Canonical Publication

A Canonical Publication is a publication that has successfully completed the Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle.

Canonical Publications possess:

- constitutional authority;
- verified engineering evidence;
- completed constitutional review;
- publication traceability;
- permanent citation status.

Future revisions shall repeat the complete Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle.

Canonical status is preserved through disciplined evolution rather than static preservation.

---

# Citation of the Canonical Laws

The Canonical Laws established in Chapter 3 are intended to become stable constitutional references.

Future engineering publications should reference applicable laws using their identifiers.

Examples include:

- CDE-001 — Engineering Traceability
- CDE-002 — Evidence Supremacy
- CDE-004 — Design Fidelity
- CDE-008 — Identity Through Metadata
- CDE-010 — Semantic Fidelity

Referencing Canonical Laws demonstrates constitutional alignment without repeating the complete doctrine.

The Canonical Laws remain authoritative within ER-CDE-001 and shall not be redefined elsewhere.

---

# Constitutional Governance

Constitutional governance preserves engineering trust by separating:

- constitutional authority;
- engineering implementation;
- independent review;
- editorial refinement;
- publication stewardship.

Each activity contributes to publication quality while preserving the independence of the others.

No single activity is sufficient to establish canonical authority.

Only the complete constitutional publication process achieves that outcome.

---

# Relationship to the Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle

This chapter governs the latter stages of the Constitutional Engineering Lifecycle established in Chapter 4.

Specifically, it defines the constitutional responsibilities associated with:

- Evidence Packages;
- Chief Architect Review;
- Editorial Refinement;
- Publication Pass;
- Canonical Publication.

Together these stages transform authored engineering knowledge into governed constitutional publications.

---

# Relationship to the Canonical Laws

Constitutional Review and Publication operationalises the Canonical Laws established in Chapter 3.

In particular, it reinforces:

- CDE-001 — Engineering Traceability;
- CDE-002 — Evidence Supremacy;
- CDE-006 — Knowledge Organisation;
- CDE-008 — Identity Through Metadata;
- CDE-010 — Semantic Fidelity.

The publication governance process therefore exists to preserve the constitutional obligations established by those laws.

---

# Enduring Principle

Engineering publications achieve enduring value only when they are constitutionally governed, independently reviewed and permanently traceable.

Constitution-Driven Engineering therefore treats publication not as the conclusion of engineering work, but as the constitutional confirmation that engineering truth has been faithfully preserved.
